Hoppers on Disney+ review: funny, emotional, and one of Pixar's easiest yeses in years

By MovieJam Crew4 min readTags: Disney+ · Pixar · Family Animation

Official Disney+ artwork used as the hero image.

Hoppers has a premise that sounds like a joke Pixar somehow committed to: a girl “hops” her mind into a robotic beaver so she can talk to animals and stop a human threat. The good news is the movie understands exactly how ridiculous that sounds and turns it into a strength. This is bright, funny, emotionally generous, and much easier to recommend than the pitch suggests.

MyMovieJam verdict: this is one of the clearest family-night yeses on Disney+ right now. It is warm without being mushy, funny without feeling desperate, and audience-friendly without becoming bland. Rating: 9.3/10.

Why it works

The film gets the balance mostly right. The animal-world comedy lands, the beaver-body hook stays entertaining instead of becoming a one-note gimmick, and the emotional core is simple enough to travel well across kids, parents, and viewers who just want one watch that does not become homework. Pixar has made bolder movies, but this one knows the assignment: be fun, be moving, and keep the momentum clean.

It also helps that the message is clear without dragging the movie into lecture mode for too long. The eco angle and local-politics friction give the story stakes, but the real selling point is watch-fit. This feels like the kind of Disney+ title you can put on for a mixed room and not spend the next 20 minutes defending your recommendation.

Best for

Family watch nights, Pixar fans who want something lighter than soul-search prestige, and anyone who needs one safe recommendation that still feels fresh.

Skip if

You want razor-sharp satire, darker animation, or a truly top-tier Pixar masterpiece that rewires your brain.

Audience pulse

The X reaction is unusually warm: lots of “beautiful,” “funny,” “I cried more than expected,” and “easy 10/10” energy. The public mood is closer to delighted surprise than polite approval.

Who should watch it

  • Parents who want one animated pick that works for the room instead of just the kids.
  • Viewers who miss Pixar making original movies that feel playful first and corporate second.
  • Anyone browsing Disney+ tonight and wanting one recommendation with very low downside.

No-sugar-coating take

Hoppers is not perfect. Some beats are predictable, some messaging is broad, and if you go in hoping for peak-era Pixar invention, this probably stops short of that level. But recommendation-wise, that is not the real question. The real question is whether this is a strong Disney+ pick for a wide audience, and the answer is clearly yes. It earns the 9.3 because it does what most people actually need: it gives you a high-confidence watch, not just an admirable one.
