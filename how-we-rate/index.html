How MyMovieJam ratings work

Our ratings are not trying to mimic a giant critic aggregator. They are built for one practical question: is this actually worth your time, and for what kind of viewer?

What the rating means

A MyMovieJam score is a recommendation signal, not an objective truth claim. It reflects the likely quality of the watch experience for the intended audience, especially in a streaming context where attention, mood, and regret matter.

9-10

Highly recommended. Strong execution, clear audience fit, and easy to recommend confidently.

7-8.5

Recommend, sometimes with caveats. Usually strong enough to be worth your night for the right viewer.

5-6.5

Mixed. May work for a narrow audience, but not a broad recommendation.

Below 5

Usually a skip unless you have a very specific reason to watch.

What we look at

  • Watchability: does it hold attention and deliver on its promise?
  • Fit: who is this actually for, and how narrow is that audience?
  • Execution: writing, pacing, tone, emotional payoff, and overall craft.
  • Streaming value: is this a good use of a night at home versus other options?

Why some scores include caveats

A movie can be decent for one audience and frustrating for another. That is why MyMovieJam often uses verdicts like “recommend with caveats” instead of pretending the same rating means the same thing for everyone.

Example:

Apex received a 7.5/10 because the tension and lead performance make it watchable, but the writing is not strong enough for a blanket recommendation.
